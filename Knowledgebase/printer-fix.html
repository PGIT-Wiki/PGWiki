Printer Troubleshooting Guide (Windows Users)

Follow these steps to identify and resolve common printer issues.

Step 1: Check the Basics

  • Make sure the printer is powered on.
  • Confirm there is paper loaded in the tray.
  • Check ink or toner levels and replace if needed.
  • Ensure all printer doors and trays are fully closed.

Step 2: Check for Error Messages

Error messages often explain exactly what's wrong.

  • Look at the printer's display screen.
  • Check your computer for messages like: Paper Jam, Low Ink, Offline, or Paused.

Step 3: View the Printer Queue

  1. On your computer, open: Devices and Printers ‚Üí Open print queue.
  2. Look for status messages like Error, Paused, or Offline.
  3. If paused, uncheck "Pause Printing" in the Printer menu.

Step 4: Restart the Printer

  1. Turn the printer off.
  2. Wait 10 seconds.
  3. Turn the printer back on and try printing again.

Step 5: Check the Connection

USB Printer: Ensure the cable is securely plugged into both devices.

Wi-Fi Printer: Check if the network light is Green. If orange or blinking, the connection is unstable.

Step 6: Clear Paper Jams

  • Open the panels and carefully remove any stuck paper.
  • Check for small torn pieces inside the rollers.
  • Note: Never pull paper out forcefully.

Step 7: Contact Support

If the issue persists, gather the following:

  • The printer model name.
  • Any specific error codes displayed.
  • The steps you have already tried.
